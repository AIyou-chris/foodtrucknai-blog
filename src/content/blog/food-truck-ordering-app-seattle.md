## Seattle's Permit Requirements (What Your App Needs to Handle)

Running a food truck in Seattle means navigating Washington State and City of Seattle regulations. Your ordering app should work with these, not against them.

**Washington State Mobile Food Unit License** — Required by the Department of Health. Issued annually. Your truck must meet health/safety standards. Your app doesn't affect this, but it should auto-calculate sales tax so you stay compliant with quarterly filings.

**City of Seattle Mobile Food Unit Permit** — Separate from the state license. Required to operate within city limits. Renewed annually. $250–400 depending on truck type.

**Sales Tax Collection (Washington State 10% base)** — Washington has no income tax but charges 10% sales tax statewide (higher in some counties). Your app should calculate and apply this automatically so you're not doing manual math at checkout.

**Tip Reporting** — If you accept digital payments (which you should), tips are reported as income. A good app tracks this automatically so tax season is cleaner.

**Parking & Event Permits** — Pike Place, Capitol Hill events, and breweries require additional event-specific permits. Your app doesn't replace these, but it makes tracking which locations you operated on much easier for permit compliance records.

**The bottom line:** Your app should remove tax/reporting friction, not add it. A tool designed for food trucks automatically handles sales tax, tracks sales history, and creates reports you can use during your annual permit renewal or if health inspectors ask for sales records.
