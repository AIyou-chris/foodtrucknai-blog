Seattle customers are used to tech. Ordering on an app isn't foreign to them — it's expected.

They order Starbucks ahead. They scan a QR code at the farmers market. They're not going to stand in your line for 20 minutes when the truck next to you has a "skip the wait" QR on the window.

If you're still running cash-only or pen-and-paper tickets in Seattle, you're playing against yourself.

## What Seattle Operators Are Actually Running

The busiest food trucks at Pike Place, Capitol Hill events, and South Lake Union lunch spots are running some version of:

**Online pre-ordering** — Customers order from their phone before they arrive. Kitchen gets the ticket automatically. No line, no shouting, no errors.

**Card + contactless payment** — Tap to pay is standard. No cash handling, no making change, faster service.

**AI upsells** — "Want to add a side?" built into the order flow. This alone adds $2-4 per ticket without any extra effort from you.

**Text/SMS notification** — "Your order is ready" keeps customers off to the side instead of crowding the window.

## The Seattle Hustle Problem

Seattle has brutal weather windows. When the sun comes out, you have 3 hours to make 8 hours of revenue.

You cannot afford slow service or order errors on a 90-person lunch rush.

Operators who've moved to digital tickets report:
- Order errors drop to near zero
- Service time per customer cuts by 30-40%
- Average ticket size goes up because upsells fire automatically

More covers per hour. Higher average ticket. Less chaos.
