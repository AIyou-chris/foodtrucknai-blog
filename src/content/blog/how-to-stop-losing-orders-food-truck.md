## The Big 4 Order Killers

### 1. The Line Walks Away

Someone sees 6 people ahead of them and leaves.

They didn't decide your food wasn't worth it. They decided their time wasn't worth it.

The fix: let people order ahead from their phone. They show up when their food is ready. No wait, no walk-offs.

### 2. The Cash-Only Problem

It's 2026. If you only take cash, you're turning away real money every single event.

The average American carries less than $20 in cash. If your plates run $12-$18, you're losing sales every time someone says "I don't have cash, sorry."

Card readers help. But a proper online ordering system — where they pay before they even get to your truck — is better.

### 3. The Handwritten Ticket Error

Someone orders a "double with no onion." You write it down. You read it wrong. The customer gets onions.

They don't come back.

Handwritten or shouted orders have a real error rate. Every error is a refund, a waste of food, or a lost customer.

Digital orders go straight to the kitchen exactly as submitted. No guessing.

### 4. No Upsell at the Window

At a restaurant, a server says "want to add fries?" At your truck, you're too busy flipping burgers to pitch add-ons.

That $2-3 upsell per order adds up fast. A truck doing 80 covers a day that upsells half of them at $2.50 earns an extra **$3,600/month**.

AI handles this automatically. The order screen asks. You don't have to.
