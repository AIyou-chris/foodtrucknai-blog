## The Big 4 Order Killers (And How to Plug Them)

### 1. The Line Walks Away ($70/day leak)

Someone sees 6 people ahead of them in your line. They have a 30-minute lunch break. They do the math and leave.

They didn't decide your food wasn't worth it. They decided their time wasn't worth it. And they're not coming back to try again tomorrow — they found another truck or grabbed a sandwich from the deli next door.

**The psychology:** Lines create perceived scarcity. Humans are impatient. Your truck window becomes a time cost, not a food source.

**The data:** On a Saturday with 80 covers, a visible line of 8+ people causes 3–5 walk-offs per hour. That's 6–10 lost orders per event.

**The fix:** Online pre-ordering eliminates the line problem. Customers order from their phone 15 minutes before they arrive. They don't see a line. They don't wait. They show up, grab their food, and leave.

Result: **+5 orders/event** = +$70/day = +$1,400/month (20 events).

### 2. The Cash-Only Problem ($35/day leak)

It's 2026. The average American carries less than $20 cash.

If your plates run $12–18, every customer who says "I only have cash and it's not enough" is a lost sale. You're leaving real money on the table because your payment system doesn't match how people actually buy food.

**The data:** 40–50% of food truck sales today come from digital payments. Cash-only operations are slower, less secure, and lose 15–20% of potential transactions.

**Quick fix:** Card readers help. But the best fix is online ordering with payment upfront. Customer pays their $15, shows up, grabs food in 10 seconds. No fumbling for cash. No "sorry, can you split this cash transaction?"

Result: **+2–3 orders/event** = +$35/day = +$700/month.

### 3. The Handwritten Ticket Error ($28/day leak)

Someone orders: "Double with no onion, extra mayo, side of chips, water."

Your crew is slammed. Someone writes it down fast. Someone else reads it. They miss the "no onion" or the "extra mayo."

The customer gets onions. They don't eat it. You remake it. You waste ingredients. They don't come back.

**The math:** Handwritten or shouted orders have 8–12% error rates (industry standard). At 60 orders/day, that's 5–7 errors per service. Each error costs you the food remake ($3–5) + the customer goodwill.

**The fix:** Digital orders go straight to your kitchen display screen exactly as submitted. No handwriting. No misreading. No errors.

Result: **Fix 2 errors/event** = Save $28–42 in remakes + keep customers happy = +$600–800/month.

### 4. No Upsell at the Window ($120/day leak)

At a sit-down restaurant, your server asks: "Want to add fries to that?" At your truck, you're too busy flipping burgers to pitch add-ons.

Your crew is focused on speed. They're not salespeople. The upsell happens to the 20% of customers confident enough to ask. Everyone else leaves without adding that $2–3 side.

**The data:** Restaurants average 12–15% upsell rates when servers ask. Food trucks with no upsell system leave $2,400–4,500/month on the table.

**The fix:** AI-powered upsells built into the ordering screen. Customer adds their main item. The system suggests: "Add agua fresca for $3?" or "Add chips and guac for $4?" They see it during checkout and often say yes. You don't have to say a word.

Result: **60 orders × $2 average upsell** = +$120/day = +$2,400/month (20 events).

## What a Tight System Looks Like

Here's the ideal workflow:

1. **Customer orders online before arriving** — They scan your QR code or click your link. No line. No wait. They see your actual menu with images and prices.
