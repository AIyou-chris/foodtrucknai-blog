## LA Food Truck Permits and What Your App Needs to Handle

Running a food truck in Los Angeles means navigating one of the more complex permit environments in the country. Here's the quick breakdown:

**LA County Mobile Food Facility Permit** — Required for any truck operating in unincorporated LA County. Renewed annually through the LA County Department of Public Health. Your app doesn't affect this permit, but make sure your digital menu matches what your facility permit covers.

**City of LA Business Tax Registration Certificate** — Required if you operate within city limits. Different from the county permit. If you work events in Santa Monica, Pasadena, or other independent cities, they have their own requirements.

**California Seller's Permit** — Required to collect sales tax in California. Your ordering app should calculate and apply California's base sales tax rate automatically — FoodTruckN AI does this — so you're not doing manual math at the end of every service.

**Event and Location Permits** — Many high-traffic LA spots (parks, farmers markets, film permit zones) require additional event permits per location. These are between you and the event organizer or city department. Your ordering app doesn't replace this — it just makes your operation at those permitted spots run cleaner.

The takeaway: your app should remove operational friction, not add compliance complexity. A good food truck app handles tax calculation, order records, and sales history automatically — all of which make your end-of-year accounting much easier.
